HPE TAS typical usage scenarios

HPE TAS is a highly capable application server platform supporting a wide range of communications applications, utilizing a wide spectrum of service scenarios and network protocols.

The potential range covers the following:

  • Highly-standardized applications such as Multimedia Telephony (MMtel), Service Centralization & Continuity (SCC-AS), IP Short-Message Gateway (IP-SM-GW) that form an integral part of the 4G and 5G voice/video telephony services, or over WiFi (VoWiFi) including supplementary services, or SMSC, and so on.
  • Highly-differentiated, non-standard telecom Value-Added Services (VAS) such as Freephone, Converged Virtual Private Network, Virtual Home Environment, Virtual PBX, IP Centrex, Premium Rate Call, Roaming Assistance, Welcome/Goodbye SMS, MultiSIM, MultiLine, IVR applications, Privacy Calling, and so on.
  • Everything in between, such as RCS applications, Voice-mail Systems, USSD-Gateway, Conference Server, or even regulatory applications such as Number Portability, anti-Wangiri, anti-RoboCalling solutions, and so on.

Supporting such a wide range of applications and services is possible thanks to the availability of a diverse set of APIs, at various levels of abstraction, a diverse set of protocol connectivity options, apart from other assistive utility functionality. However, not all APIs and not all protocol connectivity options are used in all deployments. For this reason, HPE TAS use-cases can be broadly classified into three categories, corresponding to three personas:

  • Call (and Session) Control persona
  • Messaging Control persona
  • Multimedia Control persona

Please note that the categories are not mutually exclusive. They have overlaps in terms of functionality, API, network/protocol connectivity and other common functionalities. The categorization helps limit the scope of installation, configuration, administration and also software licensing to just what is required. However, it is possible to have HPE TAS deployed and used with a mix of the previously listed personas as well.

Note: For more details on the functionalities supported under each persona, refer HPE TAS External Functional Specification document.
