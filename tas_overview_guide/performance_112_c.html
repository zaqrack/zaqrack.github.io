Performance

HPE TAS is designed to maintain its high-performance operation as a key objective. For telecom application softwares, high-performance translates to several aspects of the performance:

  • Throughput: For HPE TAS it is measured in Busy Hour Attempts. This may be converted to peak Calls per Second, peak Messages per Second, peak Transactions per Second, based on the precision of information available about the traffic and applications.
  • Latency: Measured in milliseconds, it is the intrinsic latency that is added by the platform itself.

For HPE TAS, performance depends on several factors such as the following:

  • Network Protocol: Binary encoded protocols such as INAP, CAP, MAP, and Diameter have higher throughput per unit blended compute power. Text-based protocols such as SIP and HTTP have comparatively lower throughput per unit blended compute power. IP transport choice between UDP, TCP, and SCTP for IP-based protocols also has an impact on throughput (and to some extent also on latency).
  • Encryption: May have significant impact on the performance.
  • Average Message Size: Larger messages, some of which may be fragmented based on transport usage, has to encode/decode a larger number of information-elements or message-fields, leading to lower throughput and higher latency.
  • Call flow: More complex call flow, usage of more complex protocol procedures, higher number of transactions per calls or sessions, larger number of legs of communication mean lower throughput and higher latency.
  • Mean Hold Time (MHT) or Average Call Duration: Higher MHT means lower throughput. This also has an impact on latency, but only at a very high call rate (CPS).
  • Application design and overall solution architecture: Non-blocking/asynchronous design and API usage yields higher throughput. Usage of distributed computing architecture for the solution may yield significant gains in throughput, but at the cost of increased latency.

Apart from the previously listed factors, throughput is impacted by JVM heap-size and other JVM fine-tuning (such as a Java garbage collection algorithms and settings).

Performance testing is performed for every major and minor release and many update releases. Performance report extracts are available, based on reference applications. Same may be requested via the HPE support team.
