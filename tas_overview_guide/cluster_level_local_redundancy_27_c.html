Cluster-level local redundancy

The HPE TAS software may be deployed in two forms/configurations based on the clustering of the HPE TAS platform nodes:

  • N+1 or N+M simplex pseudo-cluster, which offers virtually unlimited horizontal scalability, without needing quorum devices, with a significantly faster and simpler software upgrade and node addition/removal but also this solution needs node-by-node management.
  • N+1 or N+M true-cluster, which offers significant horizontal scalability but within certain engineering limits. It needs quorum function and offers centralized node management, with a somewhat more involved software.

N+1 or N+M refers to “N” quantity of platform nodes that are just enough to handle the expected traffic at the highest peak, with respect to the operator-specified/mandated usage and resource-reservation requirements. The “+1” or “+M” refers to one or more additional nodes, that are able to provide redundancy when one (or more) servers out of the “N” servers suffer outage. This ensures high availability of services running on the HPE TAS platform.

For SIP, a frontend load balancer (SNF) may be deployed to distribute traffic towards HPE TAS nodes. Whenever SNF is used as a frontend load balancer, it must be deployed as high-availability pair. In an IMS network, it is also possible to deploy HPE TAS SIP nodes without a frontend load balancer. This mode utilizes SIP DNS procedures (RFC3263) to distribute traffic on the downstream legs, and requires the upstream elements to also use the same DNS procedures to distribute traffic towards TAS nodes.

Note: Please see SIP Server Locator section in HPE TAS External Functional Specification document.
The M3UA frontend and load balancer (USPM) of HPE TAS are always deployed in high availability pairs. For most deployments one pair is usually sufficient, however when more required, additional pairs are can be used.
Note: Unlike SIP and M3UA/SIGTRAN traffic, HPE TAS has no dependency on a frontend and load balancer for DIAMETER traffic due to the inherent nature of the protocol, how clients address route traffic to servers, and the redundancy/reliability provisions built into the protocol. However, based on the dimensions of the DIAMETER traffic, or the need to simplify the network topology, it may be desirable to have a Diameter Routing Agent playing such a role. If required, HPE is able to propose an HPE eIUM based Diameter Routing Agent (HPE eIUM DRA). HPE eIUM DRA is a standalone product requiring its own dimensioning, deployment, software delivery, and licensing, however HPE TAS is integrated with HPE eIUM DRA, making a project-specific integration a quick, simple and risk-free exercise.
